Strategy Engine

ML Model: the calibrated win-probability picks the side and enters when confidence ≥ Min Confidence and edge (P − ask) ≥ EV Margin; sizing is fractional-Kelly capped at Risk Value. The Max Entry Price / Min Entry Minute / Min Lead controls above apply to Technical Gates mode only. Model runs in shadow until its recorded P beats the market ask on 3–4 weeks of live odds — validate before going live.

ML Exit (model mode)

The same P(up) decides exits: take-profit sells when the market's bid overpays vs the model's fair value by ≥ margin; stop sells when the win prob falls below the threshold. Prior backtests showed early exits lose to holding — leave on shadow (logs to logs/ml/exits.csv) until it proves it beats hold-to-expiry.

When on, an open position is closed (not reversed) once the 1m HA and the 1m AO have both flipped against it for ≥ Reversal Bars consecutive bars (e.g. 3) — holding UP, both red ×3 → sell the UP and go flat. The bar count avoids closing on a single-bar flicker. It only closes (never reverses), and the window is then locked for the rest of the 15m (one entry per window). Sells into the book in live mode.

Take-Profit / Stop-Loss (% of stake)

Two independent toggles. The open position is marked to market at the held side's best bid (what selling right now actually yields), and P&L is measured as a % of the amount staked: (shares × bid − stake) / stake. With a $10 stake and 30%: Take-Profit sells once the position is worth ≥ $13, Stop-Loss sells once it drops to ≤ $7. These are hard risk rules — they are checked before the ML exit and the 1m reversal-close, and they apply in both strategy modes. The window stays locked afterwards (one entry per window), so the bot will not re-enter. Live P&L is shown on the open trade on the dashboard.

Both ship OFF. This bot's own trade log showed early exits losing to holding to expiry (held-to-expiry +$699 vs signal-flip −$209 and reversal-close −$62), so arm these deliberately and watch the results.
